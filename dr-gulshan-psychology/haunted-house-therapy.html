Haunted House Therapy™

You don't need an exorcist. You can reclaim it — one room at a time.

What Is It?

Haunted House Therapy™ is my signature method for supporting trauma healing. It's designed for adults navigating complex trauma, fragmentation, or long-unspoken pain. This method is especially helpful for adults navigating PTSD, C-PTSD, creative burnout, or long-held perfectionism — the kind that keeps parts of yourself locked away.

Imagine your inner life as a house: some rooms are bright and lived in, others feel shut tight or echo with shadows. Some doors stay closed for years. Some memories show up like ghosts.

"But here's the thing: This house is yours. You don't have to fix everything at once. You don't have to be fearless to begin."

Why This Metaphor?

Locked Rooms

The parts of ourselves we've learned to avoid, fear, or question.

Ghosts

Memories or emotions that return uninvited, even when we try to forget.

Cracks in the Walls

The ways we adapted to survive — even if they leave things feeling shaky now.

Lanterns

The tools, insights, and support that help us move forward with safety.

It's about taking your time. Exploring with care. Room by room, light by light. Until it feels like a place you can live in again.

How It Integrates

Haunted House Therapy™ isn't a one-size-fits-all method. It's an adaptive, evolving approach shaped by your pace, your story, and your needs.

  • CBT: To name the beliefs and patterns that quietly shape how you move through the house.
  • CPT: To gently explore how trauma has shaped your story.
  • Mindfulness: To stay grounded in the present.
  • Positive Psychology: To reconnect with your strengths and values.

Why "Haunted"?

"Haunted" doesn't mean cursed. It means something lingers. A feeling. A memory. A reaction that doesn't quite match the moment you're in.

In Haunted House Therapy™, we don't treat these echoes as enemies. We treat them as messengers.
