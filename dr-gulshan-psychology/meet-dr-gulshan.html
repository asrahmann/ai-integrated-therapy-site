My Philosophy

I don't believe in one-size-fits-all therapy.

Your healing journey is uniquely yours, and my role is to walk alongside you—collaborative, compassionate, and unafraid to go deep. Together, we'll explore the survival strategies that helped you succeed, and gently untangle the ones that are no longer serving you.

I'll never ask you to give up your ambition, creativity, or edge. Instead, we'll work toward integration—so you can thrive as your whole self.

Creator of Haunted House Therapy™

I developed Haunted House Therapy™ as a metaphor-rich method of trauma healing—designed especially for people who feel fragmented, spiritually disconnected, or haunted by what they can't name.

Learn More About the Method

"We move at your pace. We bring a lantern, not a wrecking ball. And we walk room by room—until you no longer feel like a guest in your own life."

A Researcher Rooted in Lived Experience

My doctoral research at Hofstra University explored the relationships among cultural identity, values, practices, and well-being in Bangladeshi individuals in the U.S.—the first quantitative study of its kind with this population. It was nominated for Hofstra's prestigious Robinson Award and is published through ProQuest.

This work continues to inform how I show up as a psychologist: grounded in evidence, shaped by insight, and guided by empathy.
